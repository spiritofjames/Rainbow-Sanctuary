Meet Stephanie Wu · Lead facilitator

Helping people co-create a better future.

Known in Chinese-speaking communities as Teacher Rongrong, Stephanie founded Rainbow Sanctuary to bring inner transformation into everyday life—supporting people to meet old patterns with awareness, reconnect with their own agency, and create change together.

Inner agency Collective practice Grounded change
Her story

A life interrupted—and consciously remade.

Hardwork. Achievement. A hard awakening through sickness. Depression. Healing. Knowing thyself, letting go. Freedom.

  1. 01

    Building & achieving

    Stephanie worked hard from childhood through her early forties—building, achieving, and holding everything together.

  2. 02

    The interruption

    A kidney tumor arrived at the same time as her mother's cancer diagnosis. Her marriage ended in the middle of it.

  3. 03

    Choosing happiness

    After another relationship ended in betrayal, she chose accountability for her own life—and happiness on purpose rather than waiting for circumstances to allow it.

  4. 04

    Sharing the work

    During the pandemic, she stayed in China and began working with people at scale—the origin of the group teaching and retreat work Rainbow Sanctuary carries forward.

Her work today

A steady centre for collective practice.

Stephanie has spent years exploring consciousness practices, group facilitation, and the relationship between inner patterns and lived experience. She also understands the realities that bring people to this work: emotional exhaustion, difficult relationships, family tension, loss of direction, and the desire to feel capable of shaping life again.

In larger remote gatherings, she serves as the lead facilitator—setting a clear intention, guiding the rhythm of the practice, noticing when participants may need more space, and helping the group move toward reflection and integration.

A lifelong intention

No one should have to carry change alone.

Her vision is not to make people dependent on a teacher. It is to create conditions in which people feel supported enough to rediscover their own capacity, contribute to one another, and bring a more conscious future into practical form.

“One person awakening is a light. Many people awakening together can help shape a culture.”

What she believes

The work returns people to their own knowing.

01

Self-discovery

Healing begins with honest recognition: seeing what is present without fear, shame, or pressure to perform.

02

Agency

The facilitator does not “fix” the participant. The work supports people in recognizing and practising their own capacity for change.

03

Collective care

Personal transformation becomes more meaningful when it strengthens how we relate to families, communities, and the living Earth.

Philosophy framework

Change moves from the unseen into everyday life.

Information→Energy→Physical reality

Lasting change begins by transforming the patterns that shape perception, energy, habits, and everyday experience—not by managing symptoms at the surface.

What we don't do

Grounded by clear boundaries.

  • We do not create fear.
  • We do not promise instant transformation.
  • We do not rely on spiritual sensationalism.
