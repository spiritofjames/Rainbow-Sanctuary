Why we exist

Inner work should change how life is lived.

Not an escape from ordinary life—a way to meet it with more awareness, agency, and care.

People often arrive carrying exhaustion, repeating emotional or relational patterns, family tension, loss of direction, or the sense that years of understanding have not yet become a different way of living. Others arrive with experience and want a more coherent path for practice, service, and leadership.

Rainbow Sanctuary brings these needs into one ecosystem: accessible group experiences, the progressive Spiral Journey, focused programs, private support, retreats, and practitioner development. Each doorway has a different depth, but the intention remains the same—to help people reconnect with their own capacity and carry that change into daily life.

How the Sanctuary works

Preparation. Practice. Integration.

Experiences are held as a process, not a moment of intensity. The team helps participants understand the format, enter with informed expectations, move through the practice with appropriate choice, and translate what was meaningful into everyday life.

  1. 01

    Prepare

    Clarify the purpose, format, practical details, boundaries, and whether the experience is an appropriate fit.

  2. 02

    Practise

    Enter a guided environment with room for reflection, participation, questions, and individual pacing.

  3. 03

    Integrate

    Bring insights back into habits, relationships, decisions, and the realities of daily life.

Co-founder & lead facilitator

Stephanie holds the centre—not the spotlight.

Known in Chinese-speaking communities as Teacher Rongrong, Stephanie brings lived experience, long-term consciousness practice, and years of group facilitation to Rainbow Sanctuary.

Her path was not built from theory alone. Years of achievement and responsibility were interrupted by illness, family crisis, depression, and the collapse of relationships she once relied on. That period became a turning point: a choice to meet her own life honestly, take responsibility for what could change, and stop postponing peace.

During the pandemic, she remained in China and began working with larger groups. Those experiences became the foundation for Rainbow Sanctuary's collective practices, retreats, and progressive learning pathways.

“They believe I am the one who heals them, but it is them doing it actually.”
Her role today

A steady anchor for the field.

Stephanie guides the rhythm and intention of group practice, observes where more space or support may be needed, and helps participants move from experience toward reflection and integration.

Her intention is not to create dependency on a teacher. It is to help people rediscover their own agency—and to develop more people capable of holding conscious spaces responsibly.
